# AMO Store Listing

## Name
Aging Tabs

## Summary (max 250 chars)
Auto-close inactive tabs with visual aging. Tabs fade as they age, then close safely to a searchable graveyard. One-click restore. Pinned and audible tabs are protected.

## Description

### The Problem
Too many open tabs? You keep them "just in case" but never go back. The tab bar becomes unusable and you can't find what you need.

### The Solution
Aging Tabs gradually fades inactive tab favicons from color to grayscale, giving you a visual countdown before they auto-close. Closed tabs are saved to a searchable graveyard where you can restore them with one click.

### Key Features

**Visual Aging**
Watch your inactive tabs slowly fade. The longer you don't visit a tab, the grayer its favicon becomes. You always see which tabs are about to go.

**Graveyard with Restore**
Every auto-closed tab is saved to a searchable list. Click to bring it back instantly. Your tabs are never truly lost.

**Smart Protection**
Pinned tabs, tabs playing audio, and tabs from your whitelisted domains are never closed. Set a minimum tab count so you never lose everything.

**Configurable**
- Set your own timeout (1 minute to 24 hours)
- Enable/disable favicon dimming or title prefix indicators
- Manage a domain whitelist
- Choose how many closed tabs to remember

**Privacy First**
All data stays in your browser. No accounts, no servers, no tracking. The extension makes zero network requests.

**Lightweight**
Pure TypeScript, no frameworks. Minimal permissions. Follows your browser's light/dark theme.

### Inspired By
This extension is a modern successor to the original Aging Tabs by Dao Gottwald (2008), which was lost when Firefox moved to WebExtensions in 2017.
